Emotional Intelligence
What is Emotional Intelligence?
Emotional intelligence (EI) is the ability to understand and manage your own emotions, and those of the people around you, both individually and in groups.
How is it different to IQ?
IQ (Intelligence Quotient) was designed to measure intellectual intelligence. It gives a score from a series of tests. Emotional intelligence can only be measured by how an individual progresses through life - developing meaningful relationships with others, their interpersonal skills and understanding, their ability to manage their own emotions, and their personal skills. There is no correlation between IQ and EI scores.
Why is it important?
People with high EQ usually make great leaders and team players because of their ability to understand, empathize, and connect with the people around them. They can monitor and control their own behavior, emotions, or thoughts, and altering them depending on the situation. Also, people with high EQ are usually motivated, they can think and plan steps leading to success.
